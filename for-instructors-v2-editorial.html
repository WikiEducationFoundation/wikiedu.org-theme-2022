For Instructors

Teaching with
Wikipedia.

A reference for educators participating in Wiki Education's program — orientations, brochures, and subject guides for you and your students.

If you're interested in adding a Wikipedia assignment to your course, please visit teach.wikiedu.org to get started. There, you'll find step-by-step guidance for teaching with Wikipedia. This page serves as a useful reference once you're participating in our program.

Our staff has been supporting the Wikipedia Education Program in the United States and Canada since 2010, helping thousands of higher education instructors run Wikipedia assignments. We've collected their experiences and feedback to put together some guidelines and training for you and your students.

Questions? Please reach out to us at contact (at) wikiedu.org.

Chapter Two

Getting students ready

Your class is about more than Wikipedia, so class time should still focus on the topic of your course. That's why we've created an online training that helps students learn the basics of Wikipedia editing.

The training takes about an hour in total, and completing it is the first step in any Wikipedia assignment.

We also offer print and online copies of three brochures for your students — Editing Wikipedia, Illustrating Wikipedia, and Evaluating Wikipedia.

Chapter Three

Subject-specific handouts

Some content areas in Wikipedia have topic-specific guidelines for contributors. This can range from specific details on sourcing and citations to ethical or legal guidelines.

Provide these handouts (available in print or online) to students if you teach in these subjects.

Art history Biographies Books Chemistry Cultural anthropology Ecology Environmental Sciences Films Genes and Proteins History LGBT+ studies Linguistics Medicine Political Science Psychology Science communication Sociology Species Women's Studies
