For Instructors

Resources for teaching with Wikipedia

If you're interested in adding a Wikipedia assignment to your course, please visit teach.wikiedu.org to get started. This page is a reference once you're participating in our program.

We've been supporting the Wikipedia Education Program in the United States and Canada since 2010, helping thousands of higher education instructors run Wikipedia assignments. We've collected their experiences and feedback to put together some guidelines and training for you and your students.

Questions? Reach out to us at contact (at) wikiedu.org.

Section 01

Resources for instructors

Before you begin, it's a good idea to familiarize yourself with Wikipedia editing and community guidelines. Our online instructor orientation will walk you through the basics. You may want to take a look at the student training, too — that way you'll see exactly what your students learn (and perhaps pick up some new editing skills yourself).

Our Instructor Basics brochure contains crucial information for designing successful assignments. Case Studies spotlights successful strategies for incorporating Wikipedia assignments. For courses that engage in theoretical thinking around Wikipedia and knowledge production, we offer a Theories handbook.

Section 02

Getting students ready

Your class is about more than Wikipedia, so class time should still focus on the topic of your course. That's why we've created an online training that helps students learn the basics of Wikipedia editing. It takes about an hour in total, and completing it is the first step in any Wikipedia assignment.

We also offer print and online copies of three brochures for your students — Editing Wikipedia, Illustrating Wikipedia, and Evaluating Wikipedia.

Section 03

Subject-specific handouts for students

Some content areas in Wikipedia have topic-specific guidelines for contributors. This can range from specific details on sourcing and citations, to ethical or legal guidelines specific to that field. Provide these handouts (available in print or online) to students if you teach in these subjects.
