§ 00 — Course description

The Wikipedia assignment brings your students' expertise into one of the most-read information resources on the internet. Students improve articles in your subject area and gain skills in research, source evaluation, writing for a public audience, collaboration, and AI literacy.

Wiki Education's support is free for instructors and their students at postsecondary institutions accredited in the U.S. and Canada, funded by generous donations. We're currently accepting Summer 2026 course pages on a rolling basis.

§ 01 — Learning outcomes

By term's end, your students will be able to:

The Wikipedia assignment cultivates a specific set of skills that transfer to research, professional writing, and digital citizenship.

LO 1 · ResearchLocate, weigh, and synthesize credible sources on a contested topic.

LO 2 · Source evaluationIdentify reliable sources and reason about their bias and authority.

LO 3 · Public writingCommunicate complex ideas in neutral, accessible prose for a general audience.

LO 4 · CollaborationWork productively with peers and the broader Wikipedia editor community.

LO 5 · AI literacyRecognize what verifiable information is — and why students write it themselves.

LO 6 · Digital citizenshipContribute responsibly to a shared knowledge commons read by millions.

§ 06 — Frequently asked

Questions, answered.

Still wondering about something? Email studentprogram@wikiedu.org.

Q.01What is the Wikipedia assignment?+
A structured project in which your students improve Wikipedia articles in your course's subject area. They learn to navigate Wikipedia's policies, evaluate sources, and write for a public audience — and their work stays live online long after the term ends.
Q.02Who is eligible for free support?+
Free support extends to instructors teaching at postsecondary institutions accredited in the United States and Canada. There are no eligibility restrictions based on discipline.
Q.03What do students learn?+
  • Research and source evaluation
  • Writing for a public audience
  • Collaboration with peers and the broader Wikipedia community
  • AI literacy and a critical eye for verifiable information
Q.04How long should the assignment run?+
We recommend a six- to twelve-week timeline for most courses. Medical writing courses contributing through WikiProject Medicine's review process are exempt from that minimum.
Q.05What class size works best?+
Individual article assignments work well for classes under 40 students. Pairs or groups work for classes of 40–100. We don't recommend the assignment for classes over 100 unless adapted into a structured group project.
Q.06Can students use AI tools?+
No. Students may not use AI tools to write or edit their text for Wikipedia. The goal of the assignment is for students to draft their own contributions to improve public access to high-quality, verifiable information.
Q.07How does my student protect their privacy?+
Students choose their own Wikipedia usernames and are not required to use their real names. We provide guidance on selecting an appropriate username during training.
Q.08Can I run the assignment in multiple courses?+
Yes. Once approved, you can run the assignment in as many courses as you like, each term, with our continued support.
Q.09How do I apply?+
Visit the Wiki Education Dashboard to create your course page. Our staff reviews applications on a rolling basis and most instructors hear back within a few business days.
