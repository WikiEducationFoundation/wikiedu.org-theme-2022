Teach with Wikipedia

This page is for higher-education instructors. For our staff page, see Wiki Education § Staff. For undergraduate students, see Wikipedia Student Program.

Not to be confused with writing about your course on Wikipedia.

★
Now accepting Summer 2026 courses. Wiki Education's program is currently accepting course pages on a rolling basis. Free for instructors and students at accredited U.S. and Canadian postsecondary institutions.
Apply now

The Wikipedia assignment is a structured higher-education project supported by Wiki Education, in which postsecondary students contribute to Wikipedia as part of their coursework.1 Students improve articles within their course's subject area, gaining skills in research, source evaluation, writing for a public audience, collaboration, and AI literacy.2

Wiki Education's staff has supported the assignment in the United States and Canada since 2010, working with thousands of higher-education instructors.3 Program support — including scaffolded training modules, a course dashboard, and direct staff guidance — is provided free of charge to instructors and their students at accredited postsecondary institutions, funded by donations.4

The recommended timeline is six to twelve weeks, with shorter timelines available for medical-writing courses contributing through WikiProject Medicine's review process. Students are required to draft their contributions themselves; artificial-intelligence tools may not be used to write or edit text submitted to Wikipedia.

How it works

Instructors create a course page on the Wiki Education Dashboard, where they receive scaffolded training modules, sample syllabi for their discipline, and a structured timeline. Students complete a self-paced training that teaches Wikipedia's policies and guidelines, source evaluation, and editing mechanics.5

Throughout the term, students select Wikipedia articles within the course's subject area, evaluate the available sources, draft contributions in a sandbox, and — following peer review — publish them to Wikipedia. The contributions remain online indefinitely, where they are read, reviewed, and refined by the broader Wikipedia community.

Student skills developed

According to the program, students develop competence in the following areas:2

  • Research and source evaluation
  • Writing for a public audience in a neutral point of view
  • Collaborative editing with peers and the Wikipedia community
  • AI literacy and the practice of verifiable contribution
  • Digital citizenship through participation in a shared knowledge commons

Frequently asked questions

What is the Wikipedia assignment?

A structured project in which your students improve Wikipedia articles in your course's subject area. They learn to navigate Wikipedia's policies, evaluate sources, and write for a public audience — and their work stays live online long after the term ends.

Who is eligible for free support?

Free support extends to instructors teaching at postsecondary institutions accredited in the United States and Canada. There are no eligibility restrictions based on discipline.

What do students learn?
  • Research and source evaluation
  • Writing for a public audience
  • Collaboration with peers and the broader Wikipedia community
  • AI literacy and a critical eye for verifiable information
How long should the assignment run?

We recommend a six- to twelve-week timeline for most courses. Medical writing courses contributing through WikiProject Medicine's review process are exempt from that minimum.

What class size works best?

Individual article assignments work well for classes under 40 students. Pairs or groups for 40–100. We don't recommend the assignment for classes over 100 unless adapted into a structured group project.

Can students use AI tools?

No. Students may not use AI tools to write or edit their text for Wikipedia. The goal of the assignment is for students to draft their own contributions to improve public access to high-quality, verifiable information.

How does my student protect their privacy?

Students choose their own Wikipedia usernames and are not required to use their real names. We provide guidance on selecting an appropriate username during training.

Can I run the assignment in multiple courses?

Yes. Once approved, you can run the assignment in as many courses as you like, each term, with our continued support.

How do I apply?

Visit the Wiki Education Dashboard to create your course page. Our staff reviews applications on a rolling basis and most instructors hear back within a few business days.
