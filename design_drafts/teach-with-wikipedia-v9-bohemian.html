— Chapter one · how it works —

A whole term, step by step.

A six- to twelve-week scaffolded program. Our staff supports you the whole way, free of charge. Medical-writing courses contributing through WikiProject Medicine are exempt from the minimum.

  1. Set up your course page

    You create your course on the Wiki Education Dashboard. We'll match you with a sample syllabus from your discipline and walk you through the timeline.

    Margin note · timing~1 hour, pre-term. Mostly answering a few questions about your class.

  2. Students complete training

    Self-paced training modules teach Wikipedia policies, source evaluation, and editing mechanics — without taking class time.

    TipThe training also doubles as a great refresher for you.

  3. Choose articles to improve

    Students pick articles in your subject area, evaluate the available sources, and post bibliographies for peer review.

    What worksArticles with thin coverage but solid sourcing. Underserved topics shine.

  4. Draft — without AI

    Students write their contributions themselves. AI tools aren't permitted for writing or editing text submitted to Wikipedia.

    ImportantThis is the heart of the assignment — it's why their work is real.

  5. Publish to Wikipedia

    Their contributions go live, where the Wikipedia community reviews them and the public reads them — indefinitely.

    The momentMost students remember this. A term paper that's actually read.

  6. Reflect & measure

    Dashboard reports surface pageviews, edits, and citations added. Students see their work's real reach.

    For your recordsEasy to fold into a course portfolio or annual review.

— Folded notes · FAQ —

Questions, answered.

Still wondering? Email studentprogram@wikiedu.org.

Note 01What is the Wikipedia assignment?unfold
A structured project in which your students improve Wikipedia articles in your course's subject area. They learn to navigate Wikipedia's policies, evaluate sources, and write for a public audience — and their work stays live online long after the term ends.
Note 02Who is eligible for free support?unfold
Free support extends to instructors teaching at postsecondary institutions accredited in the United States and Canada. There are no eligibility restrictions based on discipline.
Note 03What do students learn?unfold
  • Research and source evaluation
  • Writing for a public audience
  • Collaboration with peers and the broader Wikipedia community
  • AI literacy and a critical eye for verifiable information
Note 04How long should the assignment run?unfold
We recommend a six- to twelve-week timeline for most courses. Medical writing courses contributing through WikiProject Medicine's review process are exempt from that minimum.
Note 05What class size works best?unfold
Individual article assignments work well for classes under 40 students. Pairs or groups for 40–100. We don't recommend the assignment for classes over 100 unless adapted into a structured group project.
Note 06Can students use AI tools?unfold
No. Students may not use AI tools to write or edit their text for Wikipedia. The goal of the assignment is for students to draft their own contributions to improve public access to high-quality, verifiable information.
Note 07How does my student protect their privacy?unfold
Students choose their own Wikipedia usernames and are not required to use their real names. We provide guidance on selecting an appropriate username during training.
Note 08Can I run the assignment in multiple courses?unfold
Yes. Once approved, you can run the assignment in as many courses as you like, each term, with our continued support.
Note 09How do I apply?unfold
Visit the Wiki Education Dashboard to create your course page. Our staff reviews applications on a rolling basis and most instructors hear back within a few business days.
